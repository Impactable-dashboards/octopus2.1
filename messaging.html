The four pillars

Your CI tool is doing CD as a side hustle. The practitioner wedge; leads to the free tier.

The offer

Free-tier first, brief for the buyer

Primary offer · Practitioner

Free-tier product signup. The +200% growth signal says the motion works; route form-averse engineers to product, not forms.

Secondary offer · Budget owner

Risk & efficiency brief + exec demo. The business case for the approval layer — reliability, governance, cost of failed deploys.

FSI offer · Compliance

Audit-ready deployment brief + governance demo. RBAC, approvals, full audit trail, SOC 2 — the auditor's-question answer.

Assets to build

The test plan

What the data tells us to test next

Messaging is a hypothesis until the data judges it. These are the tests the findings point to, each with a clear read.

Practitioner routing: free-tier vs form

Hypothesis: routing practitioners to free-tier signup beats the form. Read: product signups per click, by seniority. This is the single biggest expected lever.

Audit-moment hook for FSI

Hypothesis: Pillar 3 outperforms generic control-plane messaging for banks. Read: engagement and reply rate on the FSI track vs the core track.

Pillar-by-persona matching

Hypothesis: Pillar 2 wins practitioners, Pillar 4 wins budget owners. Read: CTR and conversion by pillar × persona.

Calm vs velocity framing

Hypothesis: "non-event / reliability" beats "ship faster" in this category. Read: head-to-head creative test on the same audience.

The message is built on research; the next round is decided by data.
