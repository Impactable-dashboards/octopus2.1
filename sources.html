The honest caveats

What we are and are not claiming

Two windows, both labeled
Revenue uses Feb 1 – Jun 30 (the reporting period), engagement diagnostics use the DemandSense Apr–Jul pull. Every figure states its window; we reconciled LinkedIn spend to the Feb–Jun definition ($82,079).
LinkedIn is understated
LinkedIn is measured at the top of the funnel and roughly a third of its influenced pipeline sits below LinkedIn's privacy thresholds. Its true contribution is higher than the direct return shows — which is why we judge the engine blended.
Branded search self-selects
The 71x branded term partly reflects buyers who were going to convert; its value is real but it is the harvest of demand created upstream, not standalone magic. That is the case for protecting the reach that feeds it.
No account-level revenue
Revenue is attributed by channel and keyword, not by named account, so the Accounts room leads with engagement and movement. Closing that last gap is the keystone recommendation.
LinkedIn frequency excluded
The frequency column in the LinkedIn export returned impossible values and was excluded; validate frequency in-platform.
April tracking disruption
A conversion-tracking gap in April affected non-brand CPA and forced a bidding rollback; tracking is restored and free-tier signups have since climbed to their year high.
ICP is inferred
The fit set is built from firmographics + ad engagement, not confirmed against Octopus's ICP definition or customer list. Some engaged accounts may be existing customers rather than net-new. Validating against their CRM is the keystone recommendation.
Movement is narrow by design
The 30-day pull contains only ~133 active accounts network-wide, so movement flags who is active this month, not a base-wide growth trend. Read it as a prioritization filter.

We show the math, label every window, and flag what we can't yet prove. That is what makes the rest defensible.
