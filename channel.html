The engine, in one line

The demand LinkedIn creates is what Google harvests at the brand term.

The single biggest revenue driver in either channel is branded search for "octopus deploy" — $4.6K returning $330K closed-won, a 71x return. Branded search grows only when more of the right people know Octopus exists, which is the job LinkedIn reach does. That is why the funnel call below is not "cut the top."

The funnel, corrected

Cut the waste, fix the leak — don't starve the reach

LinkedIn's stage allocation looks tilted to cold top-of-funnel, and an earlier read said "shift budget to the bottom." That read was half right. The waste is real — but the reach is what feeds the 71x branded term, and the bottom of the funnel has a conversion leak, not a funding gap. The corrected move is sharper.

Fix the leak · not a funding gap

The BOF lead form is broken

In June, 100+ form opens produced a single completed lead. A cheap headline cost hides a completion failure. Funding BOF before fixing the form pours budget into a leaky stage.

Cut the waste inside TOF

Entry-level absorbs ~42% of spend

Entry-level titles take roughly 42% of LinkedIn spend for a handful of leads, and two ad sets are oversaturated. This is the budget to reclaim — not the reach itself.

Protect the reach

TOF feeds the 71x brand term

Cold reach at 2–4x benchmark CTR is what creates the branded demand Google converts. Reclaim waste into higher-quality TOF reach, not away from reach.

Route the practitioner

Free-tier, not the form

Send the practitioners who click to the free tier (already +200%), not the broken form. That is the bottom-funnel destination that actually converts them.

The corrected move: reclaim the entry-level and oversaturated waste inside LinkedIn, fix the BOF lead form (length, fields, mobile), and redeploy into (1) higher-quality TOF reach that protects the brand-demand flywheel and (2) the practitioner free-tier path. Reach preserved, waste gone, conversion fixed — better than a blunt shift to the bottom.
