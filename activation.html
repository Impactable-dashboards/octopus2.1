The sequences

Relationship-first, value before ask

Practitioners never get a hard pitch; decision-makers get the business case; FSI gets the audit moment. LinkedIn and email mirror each other so the channels reinforce.

Practitioner Champion

Route to free tier
LinkedIn: connect (no pitch) → engage → Argo-at-scale guide → GitOps report → free-tier CTA
Email: "your CI tool is doing CD as a side hustle" → guide → data → free-tier → breakup
Pillar 2: CD is a discipline.

Budget Owner

Route to demo / brief
LinkedIn: connect → business-case DM → analyst/customer proof → risk & efficiency brief CTA
Email: business risk → ROI brief → enterprise proof → exec demo → breakup
Pillar 4: deployment as a non-event.

Regulated / Compliance

Route to audit brief
LinkedIn: connect → audit-moment hook → governance proof → audit brief CTA → governance demo
Email: the auditor's question → audit brief → FSI proof → governance demo → breakup
Pillar 3: deployment you can prove in an audit.
The FSI audit-moment track

A dedicated track for the warming banks

Why it gets its own track

Proving deployment governance to an auditor.

The banks are the accounts warming now — JPMorganChase, Capital One, Mastercard, Wells Fargo, Deutsche Bank, BNP Paribas, Bloomberg, Revolut. They share one pain no competitor markets to. This track leads with the hook "could you prove who deployed what, where, and when before an auditor asks?", targets the compliance persona alongside the budget owner, and layers ABM ad retargeting as air cover on the same accounts the client's July compliance search campaigns are chasing.

Octopus owns: 1:1 human outreach

  • Direct SDR and sales outreach on the ~20 Tier-1 named accounts
  • Carries the brand, product depth and relationships

Impactable owns: the activation engine

  • Enrichment, sequences, nurture execution across LinkedIn and email
  • ABM ad air cover and reporting; can run outreach where the client is resource-constrained

Octopus goes deep on Tier-1; we run one-to-many across the full 122 fit set.
