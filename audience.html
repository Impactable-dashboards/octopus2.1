The headline

Two conversion signals, two buyers

The account produces two very different kinds of conversion, and telling them apart is the whole game. The higher-intent signals — product and opportunity events — skew to practitioners, engineering and senior individual contributors. The lead-form leads skew to Director+ decision-makers, but also collect off-ICP small-company and business-development noise. Read together they confirm a two-tier buying motion: practitioners adopt, budget owners approve.

Higher-intent signal

Practitioners & engineering

Product signups and opportunity conversions concentrate in Platform / DevOps / SRE practitioners and senior ICs. This is the free-tier audience — and the PLG pivot's core.

Lead-form signal

Director+ decision-makers

Form leads skew Director, VP and CXO — the approval layer. CXO is the most efficient decision-maker lead at $162; Director volume is highest at 40 leads.

The verdicts

Scale, modify, dial back

Double down

Two-tier motion · Engineering & IT · US, Singapore, Canada · financial services. Route practitioners to free-tier product signup.

Modify

Budget-owner targeting is capturing small-company founders, not enterprise leaders — layer size + title. Fix the UK conversion path. Strip vanity leads from CPL.

Dial back

Off-geo waste tail (e.g. 611 clicks / 0 leads from one off-market country), off-function, off-title, and sub-50-employee companies.

Your pivot is aimed at the right people. The data agrees with the instinct — now route them correctly.
